Dōbutsu Shōgi, solved

A 3×4 children's shogi, played to the last move.
About

What this is

Dōbutsu Shōgi (動物将棋, “animal shogi”) is a 3×4 children's shogi. It is strongly solved: with perfect play the second player wins in 78 plies. This explores all 213,993,386 reachable positions under perfect play.

How to read it

winning drawing losing

The verdict bar shows who wins and in how many plies; the list ranks every legal move by result, best first. Dots on each piece show how it steps. Drag a piece or click a move; ◀ ▶ step through the line you've played.

How it was built

Computed from scratch (a Rust retrograde solver), verified against clausecker/dobutsu and served from a 333 MB table. Code & write-up.

Animal glyphs: Twemoji (CC-BY 4.0). Game first solved by Tetsuro Tanaka (2009).
